Description:
Abstract:
This coverage includes polygons and polygon labels that hold name of tectonic elements from the original tectonic map of Bangladesh.
Purpose:
To show general tectonic elements of Bangladesh. The data provide government, geological community, consultants, and the general public with  an important geologic base for small scale natural resource surveys, exploration and analysis.

Data_Quality_Information:
Attribute_Accuracy:
Attribute_Accuracy_Report:
All the attributes other than standard Arc/Info attributes are of character type.
- Attribute item "Name" was abstracted from insert on geologic maps denoting major tectonic elements.
- Standard verification of attribute reliability was performed by comparison with original insert.
- Plots were created of the coverage, colored and labeled by item "Bouguer" at the same scale, projection and map extent as the source maps for the purposes of review and examination. Review was conducted by persons uninvolved in the compilation.
Logical_Consistency_Report: Has been made by comparison with original insert.
Completeness_Report:
This map has been compiled from the small scale (1: 5,000,000) insert on Md. Khurshid Alam, A.K.M.Shahidul Hasan, and Mujibur Rahman Khan (Geological Survey of Bangladesh), and John W. Whitney (United States Geological Survey), 1990, Geological Map of Bangladesh: Geological Survey of Bangladesh Publication, scale 1:1,000,000.


1. Original insert was scanned on large format scanner in gray-scale mode with resolution 200 dpi.
2. The scanned image was transformed to Lambert Conformal projection by ArcInfo REGISTER and RECTIFY utilities.
3. Reference data for transformation were Bangladesh state boundaries taken from paper map and compared with the same boundaries on master geological map. Overall RMS error of transformation was 1500 m (0.3 mm on original paper map).
4. On-screen digitization was performed using a rectified image as a backdrop in ArcInfo ARCEDIT.
5. Tectonic element names were assigned to "Name" item of Feature Attribute Table (FAT) of "b_tecton" coverage.
6. ArcInfo coverage was converted into .E00 files, then imported to ArcView by IMPORT 71 utility and saved as shape files.

Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
1. Original map is of 500 m. maximum accuracy (0.1 mm on paper map insert of 1:5,000,000 scale).
2. Scanned images are of 200 dot/inch resolution (0.1 mm on paper). Therefore fuzzy tolerance in ARC/INFO was set to 750 meters (three pixels).
3. Several steps were taken to vectorize scanned images as follows:
3.1. Country coverage "cntry95" was used as a reference for ArcInfo REGISTER and RECTIFY programs. Overall RMS error was 1500m. The vectorized coverage was edited and attributed in Arcedit using the gray-scale image of the source map as a background guide.
3.2 Line attributing of the ARC/INFO coverage was verified and corrected by comparing it with the color image as a background. ESRI's ArcWorld 1:3M data set is too small in scale for position accuracy estimation since original map was of 1: 1,000,000 scale, but to estimate relative positional accuracy such a comparison has been performed. Overall relative position error was estimated by randomly selecting a set of locations on the ESRI World country coverage and comparing it with the state boundaries on the final geology coverage (b_geo). RMS error is 2.9 km (0.58 mm of the original paper map).
